The Best Banana Bread Recipe

The Ultimate Moist Vanilla Banana Bread with Sour Cream

Moist, tender, and packed with banana flavor. Frozen bananas work great too!

INGREDIENTS

  • 3½ cups (437.5g) all-purpose flour (spooned and leveled)
  • 2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ⅓ cup (66g) unsalted butter, melted and cooled (or use oil or browned butter)
  • ⅖ cup (40g) granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup (160g) packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 large or 4 medium very ripe bananas (about 1 lb/450g total), mashed (about 1½ cups)
  • 1 cup (227g) sour cream, full fat
  • Optional: 1 tsp almond extract (extra banana flavor)
  • Optional: ½ cup pecans or walnuts, chopped

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Prep
    • Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C)
    • Grease and line a standard 9×5-inch loaf pan (I like to grease the bottom and sides)
    • Line another baking sheet with foil or parchment (for cooling later)
    • Melt the butter in a small microwave-safe container, let cool to room temp (it's better than lukewarm)
  2. Mix Dry Ingredients
    • Whisk the all-purpose flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt into a medium sized bowl
    • Set aside
  3. Mix Wet Ingredients
    • In a large mixing bowl, beat the cooled melted butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar for about 2 minutes, until fluffy
    • Add eggs one at a time, mixing well between each addition (don't overbeat after eggs)
    • Beat in vanilla extract and almond extract (if using)
  4. Combine Wet & Dry
    • Mix in the dry ingredients on low speed until combined (don't overmix)
    • Scrape down the bowl (this is important, especially after adding heavy ingredients like bananas)
    • Fold in the mashed bananas and sour cream (use a rubber spatula or spoon on medium-low speed)
  5. Bake
    • Pour batter into the prepared loaf pan
    • Bake for 50-60 minutes, until toothpick tester inserted in the center comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs (don't overbake, or it will be too dry and crumbly)
    • Make sure you rotate the pan halfway through baking
    • Do not open the oven door during the first 40 minutes, or the top will fall
  6. Cool
    • Remove from oven and let cool in the pan for 10 minutes
    • Turn out onto a wire rack (if needed, gently run a knife around the edges to loosen from the pan)
    • Let cool completely before slicing (at least 2 hours for storage and slicing)

STORAGE

• Store in an airtight container on the counter for up to 1 week

• Wrap individually and freeze for up to 6 months (thaw on counter or microwave briefly)

• Wrap individual loaves tightly in foil and freeze (they taste like they just came out of the oven!)

• Can keep in a paper bag at room temp for up to 2 days (crouton-like texture for toasting)

VARIATIONS

  • Add chocolate chips (½ cup) or nuts (½ cup) to dry ingredients
  • Make chocolate banana bread (replace ½ cup flour with cocoa powder)
  • Add caramel drizzle or cinnamon butter on top

NOTES & TIPS

  • Don't overmix once you add the wet ingredients, or the bread will be tough
  • Very ripe bananas (black spots) = maximum banana flavor
  • Freeze bananas first (slice, freeze until rock hard, then mash) for easy storage
  • Sour cream = keeps it moist and tender (Greek yogurt works too)
  • Don't open the oven door early or the top will fall
  • The smell of baking banana bread = happiness (share with friends!)
  • Best on day 2 when flavor peaks and texture is ideal
  • Freeze individual slices in a Ziploc bag for easy snacking
