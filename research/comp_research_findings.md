# Research on Physician and APP Compensation Trends (2024-2026)

## 1. Physician Compensation Trends (2023-2026)

According to the Doximity 2024 Physician Compensation Report [https://www.doximity.com/reports/physician-compensation-report/2024], U.S. physician compensation has seen varied growth patterns across specialties and regions. Key findings include:

- National average compensation increased modestly year-over-year, with significant variations by specialty
- Cost of living adjustments are crucial factors impacting real earnings
- Regional differences show certain metro areas offering higher adjusted compensation due to market demands
- Seven of the top 10 highest-compensated metro areas in 2023 remained in the top 10 for 2024, including Las Vegas, New Orleans, and Jacksonville

The MGMA 2024 Provider Compensation Report [https://www.mgma.com/getkaiasset/252744ee-c63b-4a96-9211-8a5d6b908b39/MGMA-2024-Provider-Compensation-Data-Report.pdf] provides comprehensive benchmarks from over 211,000 physicians and APPs, showing:

- Specialty-specific compensation ranges for 2023-2024
- Productivity metrics (wRVU) influencing compensation structures
- Impact of market conditions on base salary and incentive pay

## 2. APP Compensation Trends (NPs & PAs) (2024-2025)

Multiple sources indicate evolving compensation patterns for Advanced Practice Providers:

- The Marit Health 2024-2025 Contract Trends Report [https://www.marithealth.com/posts/nurse-practitioner-and-pa-contracts-key-terms-and-2025-compensation-trends] notes:
  - 14% year-over-year salary growth for NPs
  - Average earnings increasing from $121,000 (2022) to $135,000 (2023)
  - Increasing use of flexible work arrangements and incentive structures

- Sullivan Cotter's 2025 Advanced Practice Provider Compensation Survey [https://sullivancotter.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Infographic-2025-Physician-Compensation-Trends-Final.pdf] reports:
  - Significant increases in total cash compensation across all APP specialties
  - Growing demand for surgical and procedural specialties showing highest growth rates (up 6-7% year-over-year)
  - Expanding role in primary care and chronic disease management influencing compensation models

- ECG Management Consultants' 2025 Physician and APP Compensation Survey [https://www.ecgmc.com/digital-solutions/survey/physician-app-compensation-survey-open-2025] provides data from 27 APP specialties showing:
  - Regional variations in compensation levels
  - Different compensation structures by specialty (salary + bonus + profit sharing)
  - Emerging trends in equity-based compensation and recruitment incentives

## 3. Major Factors Driving Compensation Changes (2023-2026)

Based on analysis of market reports from Doximity, MGMA, and Sullivan Cotter:

1. **Supply/Demand Imbalance** - The American Association of Medical Colleges (AAMC) projects physician shortages of 46,900–90,000 by 2034 [https://www.aamc.org/data-reports/physician-shortage-projections], creating competitive markets for certain specialties

2. **Healthcare Consolidation** - GAO reports on physician consolidation show increased market power affecting compensation structures [https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-25-107450], with hospital-employed physicians earning different compensation models compared to private practice

3. **Value-Based Care** - Market analyses indicate shifting compensation models tied to quality metrics and productivity [https://www.beckersphysicianleadership.com/news/the-new-economics-of-physician-pay-10-trends/], with some systems moving toward productivity-based adjustments rather than pure volume metrics

4. **Burnout and Workforce Sustainability** - Doximity's 2024 report notes that compensation is increasingly used as a retention tool, particularly for specialties with high burnout rates
