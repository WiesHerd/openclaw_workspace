## Key Statistical Observations

### 1. Compensation Growth Deceleration
Physician compensation growth slowed from 5.9% (2022-2023) to 3.7% (2023-2024), suggesting market stabilization or employer resistance to continued rapid increases.

### 2. Geographic Polarization
- West Coast and Northeast command premium compensation (105-113% of national median for APPs)
- Southeast and South Central lag (95-99% of median)
- Cost-of-living adjustments significantly alter rankings, with Midwestern markets (Rochester, MN; St. Louis, MO; Oklahoma City, OK) offering superior real compensation

### 3. Specialty Stratification
- Surgical and procedural specialties maintain 60-100% compensation premium over primary care
- Hospital-based specialties (especially for APPs) show strongest growth rates
- Primary care remains structurally disadvantaged despite critical role in healthcare access

### 4. APP Market Dynamics
- Compensation growth stabilizing after multiple years of steep increases
- Hospital-based APPs experiencing strongest growth (6.2% base, 5.7% TCC)
- Rural areas struggle with compensation parity (92% of national median)
- Pay compression emerging as organizational priority to address workforce shortages

### 5. Rural Access Challenge
Rural locations show 8% compensation deficit compared to urban areas for APPs, contributing to persistent rural healthcare workforce shortages. Organizations face difficult trade-offs between compensation levels and workforce distribution.
